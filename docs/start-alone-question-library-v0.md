# Start Alone Question Library v0

**Project:** Before We Build  
**Path:** Start Alone / personal preparation before serious relationship decisions  
**Status:** Draft question library for review  
**Created:** 2026-05-13  
**Inputs:** `docs/start-alone-audience-path-spec.md`, `wiki/concepts/public-language-boundaries.md`

## 1. Purpose

This library gives the first 39 questions for a Christian brother or sister who begins alone: not yet in a serious relationship, not ready to invite another person into the process, or wanting to prepare before God without haste.

The questions are meant to help a person name hopes, expectations, responsibilities, unclear areas, and topics for future honest conversation. They are not a dating quiz, not a clinical questionnaire, not a readiness score, and not a way to find or guarantee a spouse.

## 2. Wording rules for this library

Use the questions as plain reflection prompts. Preserve these rules when translating or implementing them:

- lead with God, Scripture, prayer, wisdom, character, responsibility, church, and counsel;
- ask about real life rather than labels, types, scores, or compatibility;
- avoid promises such as finding a spouse, securing marriage, or receiving certainty;
- avoid diagnostic language such as trauma profile, attachment style, psychological readiness, or personality type;
- keep answers under the user’s control; nothing is shared automatically;
- let the final map show topics for conversation, not a verdict about the user or a future relationship.

## 3. Suggested answer style

For the first version, each question can accept a short free-text answer. Later versions may add optional gentle prompts such as:

- `clear_to_me` — the user can answer clearly;
- `unclear_or_needs_counsel` — the user is unsure or wants counsel;
- `hard_to_answer` — the question touches a difficult area;
- `not_relevant_now` — the topic is not relevant yet.

Do not display these labels as a score or judgment. They are only for helping the Personal Preparation Map separate clear answers from areas needing prayer, conversation, or counsel.

## 4. Question groups

### A. Faith and foundation before God

1. What does it mean for you to bring a serious relationship decision before God rather than only following emotion, pressure, or fear?

2. Which Scripture truths should shape the way you think about love, marriage, faithfulness, responsibility, and service?

3. Where do you need God to search your heart before you move toward a serious relationship?

4. What convictions from Scripture would you not want to compromise in a future relationship?

**Scripture-near themes:** firm foundation; love that is patient and truthful; wisdom; testing what is good; humility before God.  
**Needs reviewer/theological safety review:** any implementation that turns these questions into a claim that the tool can discern God’s will for the user.

### B. Motives, hopes, fears, and loneliness

5. What hope for marriage or family is good and honest before God, and what part of that hope may need patience or surrender?

6. Where might loneliness be pushing you faster than wisdom?

7. What fears are you bringing into relationship decisions: fear of being alone, fear of choosing wrongly, fear of rejection, fear of conflict, or something else?

8. What expectations have you received from family, church, culture, or past experience that you should examine carefully?

**Scripture-near themes:** patient hope; guarding the heart; truthfulness; wisdom instead of haste.  
**Implementation note:** do not shame loneliness; name it gently as a real burden that should not become the driver of promises.

### C. Character, repentance, responsibility, and teachability

9. What fruit of character do you believe should be growing in you before you ask another person to build life with you?

10. Where might you need repentance, apology, or change before entering a serious relationship?

11. How do you usually respond when a trusted believer corrects you?

12. What responsibilities are already yours today, and how faithfully are you carrying them?

**Scripture-near themes:** humility; repentance; faithfulness in little things; teachability; bearing fruit.  
**Needs reviewer/theological safety review:** wording around repentance should invite honest self-examination, not produce false guilt or spiritual pressure.

### D. Church, counsel, and Christian community

13. Who are the mature believers, pastors, mentors, parents, or trusted friends who can speak honestly into your relationship decisions?

14. What would your church community already know about your walk with God, service, habits, and relationships that you should not ignore?

15. Are you willing to invite wise counsel before making serious promises, not only after problems appear?

16. If you were interested in someone, what would be a wise and respectful way to involve trusted believers without gossip, pressure, or control?

**Scripture-near themes:** counsel; church as body; humility; accountability; care without control.  
**Implementation note:** adapt family/parent language carefully for users from unsafe or non-Christian family contexts.

### E. Marriage expectations and daily life

17. What do you believe marriage is for before God: only comfort and companionship, or also covenant faithfulness, service, holiness, and shared responsibility?

18. What daily-life expectations would need honest discussion before marriage: home, work, rest, hospitality, health, habits, church rhythm, or time with family?

19. What would make you feel loved in ordinary life, and how do you tend to show love in ordinary life?

20. What expectations about husband and wife, leadership, support, and shared decisions need Scripture, counsel, and clear conversation?

**Scripture-near themes:** covenant faithfulness; love in action; service; responsibility; household wisdom.  
**Needs reviewer/theological safety review:** question 20 touches doctrine and church tradition; public implementation should allow church-context differences without making the tool a teacher of disputed positions.

### F. Conflict, forgiveness, communication, and reconciliation

21. When you are hurt, do you tend to withdraw, attack, pretend nothing happened, seek peace too quickly, or talk honestly? What should a future person know about this?

22. What does forgiveness mean to you, and what does it not mean?

23. How do you want hard topics to be handled before they become bitterness, silence, or repeated conflict?

24. What past patterns of conflict in your life should be brought into prayer, counsel, or careful conversation before marriage?

**Scripture-near themes:** patience; truth in love; forgiveness; reconciliation; guarding against bitterness.  
**Safety note:** if an answer suggests coercion, abuse, serious danger, or self-harm, the product should point to safe human help rather than generating ordinary relationship advice.

### G. Money, work, time, and responsibilities

25. What are your current responsibilities with money, work, study, debt, giving, saving, or family support?

26. What money habits or expectations would be hard but necessary to discuss before marriage?

27. How do you understand work and provision before God: as service, responsibility, identity, pressure, or something else?

28. How do you spend your time now, and what would need to change if you were preparing to build a shared life with someone?

**Scripture-near themes:** stewardship; diligence; generosity; responsibility; honesty in practical matters.

### H. Service, calling, and shared direction

29. Where are you already serving in church, family, work, or neighborly care, and what does that reveal about your responsibilities?

30. What kind of service or shared direction would you hope to build in a future family, without turning your hope into pressure on another person?

31. What commitments would be difficult for you to change if a future relationship required sacrifice, patience, or adjustment?

32. Where do you need to distinguish between a real calling, a personal preference, and a dream that still needs testing?

**Scripture-near themes:** one body and many members; service; sober judgment; testing what is good; humility about gifts and plans.

### I. Children, family, and future direction

33. What hopes, fears, or convictions do you have about children, parenting, family life, and spiritual care in the home?

34. What family patterns from your upbringing would you want to continue, and what patterns would need wisdom, repentance, boundaries, or healing?

35. What should be discussed before marriage about relationships with parents, relatives, future children, church community, and household responsibilities?

**Scripture-near themes:** generational faithfulness; honoring family without idolatry or control; responsibility for children where God gives them.  
**Needs reviewer/theological safety review:** avoid implying that every marriage will have children or that childlessness makes a family less faithful.

### J. Boundaries, purity, consent, safety, and the next wise step

36. What boundaries would help you honor God and another person before promises are made?

37. How will you recognize the difference between wise patience and fear-based avoidance?

38. What topic from your answers should you bring to prayer, Scripture, and wise counsel before taking a next step toward a relationship?

39. What is one concrete next step you can take without haste: continue reflection, speak with a trusted believer, seek counsel, pause, apologize, clarify a conviction, or prepare a future conversation question?

**Scripture-near themes:** holiness; self-control; honoring another person; wisdom; counsel; responsible next steps.  
**Safety note:** consent and safety language should be concrete, gentle, and non-graphic; do not spiritualize pressure or coercion.

## 5. Optional short first set

If the first screen should feel very small, begin with these 8 questions and unlock the rest gradually:

1. What are you asking God to make clear before you move toward a serious relationship?
2. Where might loneliness be pushing you faster than wisdom?
3. What convictions from Scripture would you not want to compromise?
4. What responsibilities are already yours today, and how faithfully are you carrying them?
5. Who can give you wise counsel before serious promises are made?
6. What topic would be hard, but necessary, to discuss before marriage?
7. What hope, fear, or expectation should be named honestly before God?
8. What is one wise next step you can take without haste?

## 7. Localization notes for Ukrainian/Russian

When localizing, avoid mixed English product terms. Prefer public wording from `wiki/concepts/public-language-boundaries.md`:

- questions before God / добрі запитання / добрые вопросы;
- conversation map / карта розмови / карта разговора;
- important differences / важливі відмінності / важные различия;
- what is still unclear / що ще не ясно / что ещё не ясно;
- wise counsel / мудра порада / мудрый совет;
- next wise step / наступний мудрий крок / следующий мудрый шаг.

Avoid public translations that sound like a psychological test, personality profile, compatibility score, ideal-match search, diagnosis, prediction, or verdict.

## 8. Review flags before implementation

Route these areas through reviewer/theological safety review before publication:

1. doctrinal language around husband/wife roles and shared decisions;
2. repentance wording, to avoid false guilt or manipulation;
3. family and parental counsel wording for unsafe or non-Christian family contexts;
4. children/family wording, to avoid promising children or shaming childlessness;
5. safety, consent, abuse, and self-harm handling;
6. any phrase that suggests the tool can know God’s will, judge readiness, or replace Scripture, prayer, church, counsel, or obedience.
