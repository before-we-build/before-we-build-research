# Start Alone Audience Path Spec

**Project:** Before We Build  
**Path:** Start Alone / personal preparation before serious relationship decisions  
**Status:** Internal implementation and content spec v0  
**Created:** 2026-05-13  
**Source plan:** `docs/consolidated-action-plan.md`, `wiki/concepts/plan.md`, `docs/hermes-kanban-action-plan.md`

## 1. Purpose

Define the first practical audience path for Before We Build: a Christian single who wants to prepare before God for serious relationship decisions, even when there is not yet a pair.

This path must be understandable without typology knowledge, without psychological framing, without a promise of marriage, and without treating the tool as a spiritual authority. It should lead the user into Scripture-first reflection, honest self-examination, wise counsel, and a clear next action.

## 2. Audience clarity test

A visitor should understand this in under 10 seconds:

> This is for a Christian brother or sister who is not yet in a serious relationship, or is not yet ready to invite another person into the process, but wants to prepare wisely before God before making serious relationship or marriage decisions.

The public-facing first-screen idea, in Ukrainian:

> Ви ще не маєте пари або ще не готові проходити шлях разом? Почніть самі: зупиніться перед Богом, пройдіть прості запитання, побачте, що вже ясно, а що ще треба обговорити з Писанням, молитвою та мудрою порадою.

Short label:

> Почати самому

Short explanation:

> Для християнина чи християнки, які хочуть підготуватися до серйозних стосунків без поспіху, страху або фантазій.

Primary button:

> Почати з простих запитань

Secondary link:

> Що означає підготуватися перед Богом?

## 3. Product promise for this path

Start Alone helps one person:

1. name hopes, fears, expectations, and areas of uncertainty;
2. see topics that are easy to avoid before a serious relationship;
3. prepare questions for future honest conversation;
4. identify a wise next step: prayer, Scripture reflection, counsel, repentance, a conversation with a trusted believer, or waiting.

It does not:

- find a spouse;
- promise marriage;
- score compatibility;
- diagnose character or readiness;
- identify an ideal match;
- replace Scripture, prayer, church, pastoral counsel, wise believers, repentance, or personal responsibility;
- require knowledge of Socionics, Psychosophy, Temporistics, types, profiles, or any hidden model.

## 4. User situation

Primary user:

- a Christian single brother or sister;
- may feel lonely, afraid of choosing wrongly, tired of shallow advice, or unsure how to prepare;
- may not have a specific person in view;
- wants marriage or serious relationship to be approached before God, not driven only by emotion, pressure, fear, or fantasy;
- may trust Scripture and church counsel more than psychological language or dating-product language.

Secondary included cases:

- someone beginning to talk seriously with another person but not ready for pair mode;
- someone recovering from past mistakes and wanting to slow down;
- someone preparing to speak with a pastor, mentor, parents, or trusted believer;
- a church worker recommending a private preparation path before any introduction.

Out of scope for this path:

- browsing people;
- matching two users;
- anonymous church-mediated introductions;
- couple comparison;
- pastoral counseling workflow;
- crisis care.

## 5. Entry flow

Recommended screen order:

1. Path card / landing section: “Почати самому”.
2. Foundation note: serious relationship decisions should be brought before God, Scripture, prayer, and wise counsel.
3. Boundaries note: this is a preparation map, not a verdict or promise.
4. Question flow: simple grouped questions, no type terms.
5. Review answers: user can edit before generating a map.
6. Personal Preparation Map: hopes, concerns, clear areas, unclear areas, conversation topics, wise next step.
7. Optional export/share: user may bring the map to a trusted believer, mentor, pastor, or future conversation.

One clear next action for the user:

> Answer the first small set of questions.

Avoid multiple competing actions on the first screen. Do not lead with “learn methodology”, “see research”, “calculate compatibility”, “build profile”, or “find someone”.

## 6. Question-flow boundaries

The question library for this path should be written as Scripture-first and life-practical reflection, not as a quiz that judges the person.

Question groups for the next card:

1. Faith and foundation before God.
2. Motives, hopes, fears, and loneliness.
3. Character, repentance, responsibility, and teachability.
4. Church, counsel, family, and community.
5. Marriage expectations and daily life.
6. Conflict, forgiveness, communication, and reconciliation.
7. Money, work, service, time, and responsibilities.
8. Children, family calling, and future direction where relevant.
9. Boundaries, purity, consent, and safety.
10. Unclear topics and next wise step.

Every question should be answerable by an ordinary Christian without typology, therapy, or product vocabulary.

Bad question tone:

- “What is your compatibility pattern?”
- “Which type are you?”
- “What is your attachment profile?”
- “What score would prove readiness?”
- “What partner profile do you need?”

Preferred tone:

- “What are you asking God to make clear before you move toward a serious relationship?”
- “Where might loneliness be pushing you faster than wisdom?”
- “What do trusted believers already see in your life that you should listen to?”
- “What topics would be hard, but necessary, to discuss before marriage?”
- “Where do you need counsel before making promises?”

## 7. Output: Personal Preparation Map

The report for Start Alone should be named internally `PersonalPreparationMap`.

Public-facing Ukrainian name:

> Карта підготовки до мудрої розмови

Required sections:

1. **What you already named clearly**  
   Hopes, convictions, boundaries, responsibilities, and concrete expectations the user has stated.

2. **What is still unclear**  
   Areas where answers are missing, vague, conflicted, or need more prayer, Scripture reflection, or counsel.

3. **Topics not to avoid**  
   Practical themes that should be discussed before serious promises: faith, church, family, money, work, conflict, service, children, responsibility, past wounds, boundaries.

4. **Questions for a future conversation**  
   Plain-language questions the user can later bring into a serious conversation with another person.

5. **Questions for prayer and wise counsel**  
   Questions suitable for a mentor, pastor, parents, or trusted believers, depending on the user’s church context and safety.

6. **One wise next step**  
   A single concrete next action, selected from safe categories such as: continue reflection, talk with a trusted believer, bring a topic to prayer, read a Scripture passage with counsel, pause before pursuing a relationship, or prepare for a future conversation.

Required caveat in report language:

> This map is not a verdict, prophecy, promise of marriage, or replacement for Scripture, prayer, church, counsel, or obedience. It is a structured help for seeing what to discuss and what still needs wisdom.

Use caveats in the report body or near the result, but do not make the first screen feel fearful or defensive.

## 8. Scripture-first posture

The path should be grounded in Scripture themes without inventing proof texts or using Bible references as decoration.

Appropriate themes:

- building on a firm foundation;
- love as patient, truthful, enduring, and not self-seeking;
- wisdom, counsel, and testing what is good;
- unity in Christ and the Body of Christ;
- responsibility, forgiveness, humility, repentance, and service;
- guarding against fear, fantasy, haste, and self-deception.

Potential references for human/pastor review, not automatic proof-texting:

- Matthew 7:24–25;
- 1 Corinthians 13:4–7;
- Colossians 3:12–14;
- Proverbs on wisdom and counsel;
- 1 Thessalonians 5:21;
- 1 Corinthians 12:13–22.

Implementation rule: if public copy makes a normative Christian claim, either keep it broad and faithful, or route it through human/pastor review before publishing.

## 9. Church and counsel boundaries

The path must clearly preserve human and church boundaries:

- The user controls their answers and whether anything is shared.
- No pastor, church worker, mentor, parent, or future partner receives answers automatically.
- Sharing should be optional, explicit, and revocable where the system supports accounts.
- Sensitive topics should invite wise counsel, not algorithmic judgment.
- If answers suggest coercion, abuse, self-harm, or serious danger, the product should not generate a spiritualized answer; it should point to safe human help and local emergency / pastoral / professional support as appropriate.
- Church-mediated introduction features are a later path and must not leak into Start Alone as browsing, ranking, or hidden matchmaking.

## 10. Internal data and AI constraints

Allowed AI behavior:

- summarize user answers;
- separate stated facts from self-descriptions and uncertainties;
- identify missing topics;
- generate follow-up questions;
- compose a plain-language preparation map;
- suggest safe next-step categories.

Disallowed AI behavior:

- declare the user ready or not ready for marriage;
- infer hidden motives as facts;
- generate a spouse recommendation;
- compute or display compatibility percentage;
- simulate a future relationship;
- tell the user God’s will;
- override Scripture, church, counsel, or conscience.

Required internal labels:

- `user_stated_fact` — what the user explicitly wrote;
- `user_self_description` — how the user describes themself;
- `unclear_or_missing` — important area not yet answered clearly;
- `possible_concern` — cautious, non-diagnostic flag needing human wisdom;
- `suggested_question` — question for future conversation;
- `wise_next_step` — one safe next action.
