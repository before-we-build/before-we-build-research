# Audience Terminology Map

## Purpose

This page defines how Before We Build should translate its research entities into language for two public audiences:

1. **Christian public audience** — especially Scripture-first / Baptist / Sola Scriptura-sensitive readers.
2. **Non-Christian or general audience** — people who may still benefit from careful conversation, role-fit, team, or relationship guidance without accepting the Christian foundation.

This page does **not** replace [[glossary-core]] or [[glossary-extended]]. It is a public-language bridge: research terms may remain in the research layer, but public pages should use audience-appropriate language.

## Governing rule

Research terms may describe internal architecture, source traditions, or experimental hypotheses. Public terms should describe what the person can responsibly do next.

For the Christian public audience, prefer language that leads to Scripture, wisdom, character, prayer, church, honest conversation, and wise counsel.

For the non-Christian public audience, prefer language that leads to clarity, responsibility, consent, evidence, conversation, and humane decision-making.

## Main entity groups in the current wiki

The current research wiki contains these major entity families:

- **Christian foundation entities**: faith, foundation, family formation, fidelity, responsibility, forgiveness, community, wisdom, counsel, fruit, character.
- **Research architecture entities**: latent process, model, persona, digital twin, simulation, hypothesis, scoring, calibration, validation.
- **Typology system entities**: Socionics, Psychosophy / Attitudinal Psyche, Temporistics.
- **System-specific entities**:
  - Socionics: information metabolism, Model A, information elements, function positions, quadras, intertype relations.
  - Psychosophy: Will / Volition, Emotion, Logic, Physics, positions, relation signatures.
  - Temporistics: Past, Present, Future, Eternity, aspect-position archetypes, full type permutations.
- **Application entities**: compatibility, relationship discernment, family, church service, team, business partnership, role fit, smart mobilization.

## Christian-side renaming recommendations

The following internal entities should be renamed or demoted in Christian-facing pages.

### Strong rename recommendations

| Current term | Recommended Christian-facing term | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Compatibility | questions before a serious step; map for conversation | Avoids verdict-like framing. |
| Test | simple questions; questions for discernment | Softer and less psychological. |
| Profile | notes for conversation; map of differences | Avoids identity labeling. |
| Type | pattern to discuss; difference to notice | Avoids reducing a person to a label. |
| Score | limited signal; do not show as headline | Avoids false certainty. |
| Model / engine | map; hidden research layer | Avoids presenting human system as authority. |
| Simulation / prediction | possible pattern to watch | Avoids oracle/providence confusion. |
| Digital Twin | do not use | Too dehumanizing for public Christian trust. |
| Research / methodology | keep deep; if needed: how we test the map | Ordinary visitors should not enter through research. |

## Non-Christian / general audience translations

The general audience does not need explicitly Christian wording, but it still needs humane, non-deterministic language.

Preferred pattern:

```text
serious decision → repeated patterns → questions to discuss → consent and evidence → provisional next step
```

Avoid:

- “the system knows who you are”;
- “the model predicts your future”;
- “ideal match”;
- “diagnosis”;
- “objectively compatible/incompatible”;
- “automated decision.”

Prefer:

- “conversation map”;
- “areas to discuss”;
- “possible tension points”;
- “working hypothesis”;
- “evidence to check in real life”;
- “human decision with consent.”

## Editorial workflow for renaming entities

When a research entity appears in Christian-facing copy:

1. Identify the source-layer term.
2. Ask whether the ordinary visitor needs the term at all.
3. If not needed, replace it with a human question.
4. If needed for transparency, put it in a deeper explanation with caveats.
5. Add biblical grounding when the wording makes a Christian normative claim.
6. Keep source traditions separate: do not merge Socionics, Psychosophy, Temporistics, or Attitudinal Psyche labels into one public truth table.

## Human-question replacements

Many internal entities should become questions rather than labels.

| Internal category | Christian public question | General public question |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic / Temporistics | Where are we going before God? What hope, fear, memory, or meaning drives us? | Where are we going, and what time horizon shapes us? |
| Operational / Psychosophy | How do we choose, carry responsibility, reason, feel, and care for practical life? | How do we make decisions, handle emotions, reason, and manage practical needs? |
| Tactical / Socionics | How do we speak, listen, understand, miss each other, and repair? | How do we communicate, process information, and handle friction? |
| Compatibility vector | What topics must we discuss before a serious step? | What areas of fit and tension should we discuss? |
| Relation pattern | Where may we help each other, pressure each other, or misunderstand each other? | Where may interaction be easy, tense, or ambiguous? |
| Role fit | Where can this person serve faithfully without being crushed or mislabeled? | What role fits this person’s strengths, limits, and support needs? |
