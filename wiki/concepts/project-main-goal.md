# Project Main Goal

## One-Sentence Goal

**Before We Build helps Christians examine the foundation before serious shared decisions: first for lonely brothers and sisters discerning relationships and marriage, and later for family, church service, teams, business partnership, and role/responsibility fit.**

It uses a hidden three-level compatibility map to turn complex differences into simple questions for prayer, Scripture reflection, honest conversation, wise counsel, and responsible next steps.

## Primary Launch Audience

The first practical audience should be:

> **lonely Christian brothers and sisters who desire marriage or a serious relationship, but do not want to move from loneliness, fear, fantasy, or superficial attraction.**

This audience may include:

- a single Christian who wants to prepare before meeting someone;
- a brother or sister who is afraid of choosing wrongly;
- someone who has repeated painful relationship patterns;
- someone who wants marriage but does not know what to examine before a serious step;
- a pair that has begun talking and needs a faithful map for important conversations;
- churches that later may want to help single believers meet safely and responsibly.

The first product path should explicitly allow a person to begin alone:

> **You can begin alone — even if you do not yet have a pair.**

The tool should help such a person name hopes, fears, values, expectations, unclear areas, and questions that should be brought before God, Scripture, wise counsel, and future conversation.

## Broad Umbrella

The launch audience is narrow, but the project should not collapse into a dating app or marriage-only product.

Compatibility is a broad word. In Before We Build, it means the practical question:

> **Can we build this together faithfully, wisely, and without hiding important differences?**

That question can apply to:

- relationship and marriage discernment;
- family life;
- church service;
- friendship and mutual support;
- teams and shared work;
- business partnership;
- role and responsibility fit;
- later research into broader cooperation and compatibility patterns.

The public doorway may begin with lonely Christian singles, but the underlying project remains an umbrella for many situations where people need to build together.

## Three-Level Compatibility Map

The research layer remains useful, but it should be hidden from the ordinary Christian public doorway.

Internally, Before We Build uses three typological systems as heuristic maps of compatibility at three levels:

| Level | Internal system | Hidden research question | Public Christian translation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategic** | Temporistics | Do people share a compatible temporal/existential direction? | Where are we going before God? What future, meaning, memory, and life direction are we building from? |
| **Operational** | Psychosophy | Can people organize decisions, pressure, effort, emotion, logic, will, and practical action together? | How do we make decisions, carry responsibility, handle pressure, and act together? |
| **Tactical** | Socionics | Can people perceive, exchange, repair, and coordinate information in concrete situations? | How do we talk, hear, misunderstand, repair, and coordinate daily steps? |

The public audience does not need to see the words **Temporistics**, **Psychosophy**, or **Socionics** at the doorway. They should see ordinary questions:

1. **Where are we going?**
2. **How do we decide and carry responsibility?**
3. **How do we talk, hear, and repair misunderstandings?**

## Christian Public Framing

The project should not be presented to Christians as psychology-first or typology-first.

Public framing should be:

- Scripture first;
- foundation before building;
- wisdom before haste;
- character before attraction;
- honest conversation before commitment;
- prayer and counsel before major decisions;
- a map of differences, not a verdict.

Suggested public explanation:

> Before We Build is a Christian path of questions before people build together. It helps a person or pair see important differences, name what is still unclear, and prepare for an honest conversation before God — first in the area of relationships and marriage, later in family, service, teams, and common work.

## What Practical Value Means

The project has practical value only if it helps people do something concrete and faithful.

For the first audience, the practical outputs should be:

- a **personal preparation map** for a single person;
- a **conversation map** for two people;
- a list of **questions before a serious step**;
- visible areas of agreement, difference, and uncertainty;
- suggested next conversations with a pastor, mentor, parents, trusted believers, or the other person;
- reminders to compare conclusions with Scripture, real fruit, wise counsel, and lived behavior.

The output should not be a compatibility verdict, spouse promise, score, diagnosis, or spiritual authority.

## Public Language to Prefer

Prefer:

- foundation;
- before we build;
- questions before a serious step;
- map of differences;
- conversation map;
- wise next step;
- prayer and counsel;
- character, responsibility, faithfulness, forgiveness;
- discernment before God;
- what still needs to be discussed.

Avoid in the first public doorway:

- compatibility score;
- ideal match;
- psychological profile;
- typology test;
- prediction;
- model;
- simulation;
- digital twin;
- diagnosis;
- verdict.

## Russian Public Wording Draft

> **Before We Build помогает христианам остановиться перед серьёзным шагом и проверить основание: сначала — одиноким братьям и сёстрам, которые думают о отношениях и браке; позже — семьям, служению, командам и общему делу.**
>
> Это не обещание найти супруга и не приговор о совместимости. Это путь простых вопросов, карты различий и честного разговора перед Богом: куда мы идём, как принимаем решения, как говорим, слышим и миримся.

Shorter version:

> **Прежде чем строить отношения, семью, служение или общее дело — проверьте основание перед Богом.**
>
> Начать можно одному: с вопросов о вере, характере, ожиданиях, страхах, ответственности и том, что ещё нужно обсудить до серьёзного шага.

## Product Direction

The first implementation should not try to solve all compatibility contexts at once.

Recommended order:

1. **Start alone** — a single Christian brother or sister prepares before a relationship.
2. **Talk together** — a pair receives a conversation map before a serious step.
3. **Church-mediated help** — later, churches may support introductions with consent, privacy, and pastoral responsibility.
4. **Broader paths** — family, church service, teams, business partnership, and role fit are added as separate routes under the same foundation.

## Guardrail

The project should always preserve both truths:

1. **Narrow launch focus:** lonely Christian singles discerning relationships and marriage.
2. **Broad future umbrella:** compatibility as a map for many kinds of faithful building together.

If the site speaks to everyone at once, it becomes vague. If it speaks only about dating, it loses the broader calling. The correct public shape is:

```text
one clear first doorway
→ lonely Christian brothers and sisters before relationships/marriage
→ broader paths shown lower and later
→ hidden research engine translated into ordinary Christian questions
```
