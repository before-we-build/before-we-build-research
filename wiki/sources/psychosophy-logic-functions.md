# Psychosophy Logic Functions — 1L, 2L, 3L, 4L

Languages: **English** | [[psychosophy-logic-functions-ru|Русский]] | [[psychosophy-logic-functions-uk|Українська]]

## Status and boundary

This page is a **Before We Build research-layer synthesis** of Logic across all four Psychosophy positions. It is not a diagnosis, spiritual verdict, moral ranking, or psychometric instrument.

In Before We Build, Psychosophy belongs to the **operational level**: it describes how a person organizes action, pressure, priorities, analysis, synthesis, and energy around concrete life processes. Use this page as a map for questions and observation, not as a verdict about a person.

## What Logic means here

**Logic / L** is the aspect of:
- thinking, argument, explanation, proof, and intellectual clarity;
- beliefs, opinions, theories, learning, and competence;
- analysis, synthesis, consistency, and truth-claims;
- conversation about what is reasonable, proven, possible, or mistaken;

Before We Build reads Logic not as a fixed human essence, but as **how the person organizes the logic sphere**: what they notice, what they prioritize, what they negotiate, what they avoid, and where they need support.

## Safety and interpretation rules

1. **Do not reduce a person to a function.** The page describes a recurring operational pattern, not the worth or destiny of a human being.
2. **Do not use the aspect to excuse sin, control, contempt, or passivity.** Responsibility remains personal and relational.
3. **Separate typology from Scripture, conscience, pastoral care, and real history.** Typology may suggest questions; it cannot judge the heart.
4. **Clinical and safety concerns come first.** Abuse, coercion, serious mental illness, trauma, self-harm, addiction, or danger require appropriate human support, not typological interpretation.
5. **Look for repeated patterns across contexts.** A single conflict, role, crisis, or habit is not enough to type position or accentuation.

## Quick comparison

| Position | Working name | Short pattern | Typical need |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1L** | Primary organizer | sets a confident standard and expects it to stand. | respect for standards plus humility toward process |
| **2L** | Cooperative organizer | works through the aspect with others in dialogue. | shared process, reciprocity, and boundaries |
| **3L** | Vulnerable processor | treats the aspect as important but exposed and uncertain. | patient support, no shaming, and concrete clarification |
| **4L** | Frame-taker | adapts to an external frame and may under-prioritize the aspect. | clear healthy structure without infantilizing |

## 1L — First Logic: primary organizer

### Core pattern

1L sets a confident standard and expects it to stand in the logic sphere. In Before We Build terms, this position shows whether the person tends to set, share, process, or receive a frame around logic.

```text
notices logic signals → sets a standard → expects a result → acts inside relationship and responsibility
```

### What 1L tends to notice

- where the logic sphere is ordered or disordered;
- whether another person respects, ignores, overuses, or pressures this aspect;
- what is being decided, discussed, feared, or left vague;
- whether the person is serving, controlling, disappearing, or becoming ashamed around this aspect.

### Strengths

- can reveal a distinct operational gift in logic;
- can help a family, church, team, or friendship see a real process they were ignoring;
- can become mature when joined with humility, counsel, repentance, and practical responsibility;
- can translate hidden tension into concrete questions.

### Risks

- may absolutize its own pattern and misread others;
- may confuse comfort, fear, confidence, or adaptation with maturity;
- may become a source of pressure, withdrawal, shame, or over-responsibility;
- may be distorted by family history, trauma, social role, illness, poverty, war stress, or spiritual immaturity.

### Examples

- states conclusions sharply: “This is true; the argument is settled.”
- Healthy form: gives clear doctrine or analysis, but may dismiss questions too quickly.
- Question to ask: where is this pattern serving love and responsibility, and where is it hiding fear, control, contempt, or passivity?

### Helpful questions

- What exactly is happening in the logic sphere: decision, discussion, fear, neglect, or pressure?
- Am I listening to the other person’s real process, or only defending my own pattern?
- What would responsibility look like here without domination or self-erasure?

## Logic accentuations

### Source status

Psychosophy accentuations are an **unofficial/community extension**, not the core Afanasyev doctrine. In Before We Build they should be treated as a weak second-layer hypothesis: first identify the aspect and position carefully, then ask which property of that function is most visible.

The accentuation layer says that every function has three properties, and one of them may be expressed more sharply than the others. For Logic, this means that two people with the same 1L, 2L, 3L, or 4L can still look different in the logic sphere.

### Position properties used for Logic

| Position | Properties |
|---|---|
| **1L** | Strong / result-oriented / introverted |
| **2L** | Strong / process-oriented / extraverted |
| **3L** | Weak / process-oriented / introverted |
| **4L** | Weak / result-oriented / extraverted |

### 1L accentuations

**1L with strong-subjective accent**

- Manifestation: especially confident that their standard in this aspect is valid in the logic sphere.
- Gift: clear strength and initiative.
- Risk: overriding other people’s process.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**1L with result accent**

- Manifestation: wants a stable result and may dislike ongoing discussion in the logic sphere.
- Gift: finishes and stabilizes.
- Risk: closes conversation too early.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**1L with introverted/principled accent**

- Manifestation: focuses strongly on their own state and standard in the logic sphere.
- Gift: integrity and self-possession.
- Risk: rigidity or poor receptivity.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

### 2L accentuations

**2L with strong-subjective accent**

- Manifestation: actively supports others in this aspect and feels competent in the logic sphere.
- Gift: generous help and competence.
- Risk: becoming the default processor for everyone.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**2L with process accent**

- Manifestation: especially values discussing and adjusting this aspect together in the logic sphere.
- Gift: excellent dialogue and refinement.
- Risk: over-processing and delayed decisions.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**2L with extraverted/adaptive accent**

- Manifestation: oriented toward others’ states and adapts easily in the logic sphere.
- Gift: empathy and flexibility.
- Risk: self-erasure.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

### 3L accentuations

**3L with weak/objective accent**

- Manifestation: feels vulnerability in this aspect most sharply in the logic sphere.
- Gift: honesty about need and limits.
- Risk: deep insecurity and comparison.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**3L with process accent**

- Manifestation: processes this aspect repeatedly in the logic sphere.
- Gift: careful clarification.
- Risk: loops and dependence on reassurance.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**3L with introverted/principled accent**

- Manifestation: sensitive about their own state and boundaries in the logic sphere.
- Gift: seriousness and conscience.
- Risk: defensiveness when touched.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

### 4L accentuations

**4L with weak/objective accent**

- Manifestation: shows low self-prioritization and can resemble visible vulnerability with less process in the logic sphere.
- Gift: simplicity and low demands.
- Risk: neglect, dependence, or passivity.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**4L with result/unconscious accent**

- Manifestation: wants a simple stable frame and may not question habits in the logic sphere.
- Gift: loyalty to routine and low drama.
- Risk: inherited or imposed frames remain unexamined.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

**4L with extraverted/adaptive accent**

- Manifestation: responsive to others and the external frame in the logic sphere.
- Gift: easy coexistence and service.
- Risk: other people’s frame replaces personal responsibility.
- Example: a recurring pattern appears across decisions, conversations, conflict, and responsibility around logic, not only in one isolated episode.

### How to use accentuations safely

- Treat accentuation as a **fine-tuning question**, not a separate type verdict.
- Do not type accentuation from one domain only; one conflict can be situational.
- Look for repeated patterns across several domains of logic.
- If abuse, coercion, self-harm, addiction, trauma, or serious illness appears, stop typological interpretation and use appropriate human support.

## Pair dynamics around Logic

### 1L + 4L

Can work when 1L provides a mature frame and 4L receives it without losing adult agency. Risk: domination plus passivity.

### 2L + 3L

Often supportive when 2L helps 3L process the aspect without shame. Risk: over-care plus dependence on reassurance.

### 1L + 3L

Can be productive but sensitive. 1L wants a result; 3L may still be processing vulnerability. Risk: pressure and perceived judgment.

### 2L + 4L

Can be gentle and cooperative. Risk: 2L carries the process while 4L remains under-developed.

## Before We Build translation layer

For public or pastoral-facing product language, do not expose “1L/2L/3L/4L” first. Translate into questions:

- How do we think, test claims, teach, learn, disagree, and admit limited knowledge?
- Where does one of us impose standards, and where does one of us disappear?
- Where is there fear, shame, pressure, or neglect around this aspect?
- What concrete conversation would protect love, responsibility, and peace?

## Non-claims

This page does **not** claim that Logic position determines:

- moral character or spiritual maturity;
- salvation, calling, obedience, or pastoral authority;
- mental health, trauma history, or clinical condition;
- marriage readiness or compatibility verdict;
- real competence in every situation;
- the person’s worth before God.

Logic can only suggest a possible operational pattern. Real people must be understood through Scripture, conscience, health, history, choices, counsel, and concrete observation.
